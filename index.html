Exploring Common Flock Threats

Poultry

There are a number of poultry diseases that can be extremely contagious and disruptive on farms, quickly infecting entire flocks and threatening commercial operations. These diseases can also be difficult to eliminate, even with careful disinfection procedures.

Understanding common infectious diseases among chicken flocks and how they spread is key to tackling them before they can impact a farm.

Infectious Bursal Disease

Infectious bursal disease (IBD) is a highly contagious immunosuppressive illness linked to increased mortality and reduced flock uniformity. Chickens infected with IBD face a much higher susceptibility to other poultry diseases.
LEARN MORE

Newcastle Disease

Newcastle Disease (ND) is a viral threat that can have a quick performance-eroding effect on entire flocks, restricting international trade. ND requires repeated vaccination to maintain long-term protection for a flock.
LEARN MORE

Infectious Laryngotracheitis

Infectious Laryngotracheitis (ILT) is a highly transmissible viral infection of the respiratory tract of chickens. It is found in all parts of the world and can quickly infect entire flocks. Because there are no treatments for ILT, preventing an outbreak by vaccination is essential.

Infectious Bronchitis

Infectious bronchitis (IB) is an acute, highly contagious upper respiratory virus that only causes diseases in chickens. The virus is found worldwide and morbidity for flocks affected by IB is typically 100%.
